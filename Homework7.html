Overview of Dataset

This dataset, baby.csv, focuses on properties of newborn babies, such as their birth weights and the number of days spent in the mother’s womb, as well as properties of the mothers themselves, such as their ages and whether they smoke.

This dataset was provided by Professor Burnett during the DATS 1001 course last semester.

Reflection on Analysis

The visualizations in this dataset focus specifically on the correlation between gestational days and birth weight for newborn babies. The observations on the lower end of the scatterplot belong to babies who are lighter in weight, while babies who are heavier in weight are on the top end of the scatterplot. The labeling for the first visualization is meant to identify where the highest and lowest weight values for the distribution are located, while the labeling used in the second visualization was meant to distinguish between babies who are born to maternal smokers or maternal non-smokers. The color scheme in the second visualization was chosen to be intuitive and understandable for analysis, with green and red corresponding to simple binary outcomes. The text representing each category of mothers (smokers vs. non-smokers) was placed directly adjacent to the predominant cluster to replace the legend as an identifier for each category. The table corresponding to this data also distinguishes gestational days for babies belonging to maternal smokers and maternal non-smokers, as well as providing a Δ value to indicate the change in the average number of gestational days for babies belonging to maternal smokers and non-smokers. The table also includes a percent change variable to indicate the amount of percent change between the means for babies of maternal smokers and maternal non-smokers. The means of the gestational days were chosen as a simple aggregate of gestational days for babies belonging to maternal smokers and non-smokers.
